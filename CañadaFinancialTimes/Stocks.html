Introduction​

Moreover, you may ask “How are stocks interpreted?” As a beginner, it is essential to get familiar with two approaches: Technical Analysis and Fundamental Analysis. These are two conditional forms to evaluate a stock’s potential. ​

Basic Terms:​

  • Bullish: In a market’s price graph, this is when the price illustrates an upward trend like the example below:
  • Assets: Resources that profit a Company such as machinery for production. In the case of stocks, investors purchase a fraction of the company, which in turn gives the company a source of profit. The stock system itself is an asset to the company/our investments are assets to a company.
  • Bearish: In a market’s price graph, this is when the price demonstrates a downward trend. ​
  • Stock/equality: A fraction/percentage of a company’s total asset security. ​ Example: “I’ve decided to sell my 1% ownership of this company because of its sudden Bearish trend. ​

Technical Analysis

Technical Analysis is the optimization of “math/statistical” tools to predict a Company’s short-term performance depending on its stock's historical value and volume on a price over time graph. Short term performance refers to brief volatile stock behaviors. While these statistical tools are used, technical analysis also evaluates a security's supply and demand.​

On the left is a graph depicting statistical tools/strategies that are frequently used to determine a company’s value

Fundamental Analysis

Fundamental Analysis primarily focuses on the social climate, a company’s production, profit, competitors, and management strategy to determine a company’s value through two different lenses; Quantitative analysis and Qualitative Analysis. ​

Quantitative Analysis is the use of various numerical data to determine a company’s value.

On the other hand, qualitative data measures a company’s value through information that cannot be evaluated numerically.

Overestimation of a company’s value could discourage fundamental analysts from investing in them while
Underestimations could attract investors. This method is often used for long-term investments. ​

Below is a graph depicting the concepts and documents that determine a company's value

Fundamental Analysis could be divided into two categories --> Quantitative and Qualitative. The listed terms below Quantitative and Qualitative are documents and methods that can be accessed and or researched to conclude the probability of a market's increase (overestimation) or decrease (underestimation). These documentations and research methods are the few of many methods that can assist an investor and analyst.​

Investment Lesson

All in all, many investment research methods are derived from Technical and Fundamental Analysis, two of which have different strategies to determine an investment of a company's security. Technical applies statistical tools (such as averages) on market price trends to predict the next short-term bullish or bearish trend. ​

On the other hand, Fundamental analysis evaluates/judges a company's long-term value based on Quantitative factors and Qualitative factors outside of a Company's price graphs. Quantitative factors include numerical data such as a company's income statement and Qualitative is concerned with data that cannot be depicted numerically such as a Company's Management procedures. ​
